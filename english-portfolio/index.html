Transferable Skills

Which transferable skills does this work demonstrate?

I used critical thinking by looking deeper into the characters, conflicts, and themes instead of just focusing on what was happening in the story. For example, Jack being stuck in a time loop is not just a little gimmick in the story, but is used to show how he avoids accepting Kate’s death and struggles with guilt and responsibility. I also analyzed different types of conflict, such as Jack vs time, Jack vs himself, and Jack vs his friends, and how each one contributed to his growth as a character. Making connections between the story and real life, such as Jack trying to control Kate’s illness, is a common way people respond to fear and loss.
This assignment also demonstrates my communication skills. I explained my ideas clearly using literary terms and supported them with quotations and page numbers. During book club discussions, I shared my thoughts in a way that others could understand and respond to, trying to make a conversation. I also made a discussion question for deeper thinking about the author’s message and the characters’ decisions.

How could you apply these skills in another context?

I use critical thinking in other classes when I need to analyze information instead of just accepting it at face value. I also use communication skills in group work by explaining my ideas and listening to others.
