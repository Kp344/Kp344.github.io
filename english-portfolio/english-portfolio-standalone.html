Learning Reflection

What did you learn?

I learned that narrative perspective is not merely a technical element but a powerful tool for meaning-making. Understanding unreliable narrators, stream of consciousness, and third-person omniscience deepened my appreciation for how authors craft their messages. I also discovered that these techniques apply beyond literature to how we communicate in everyday contexts.

How did this learning influence your perspectives?

This learning transformed my perspective on storytelling and communication. I now recognize that every narrative choice carries weight and intention. I've become more conscious of how media, news, and even conversations are shaped by the perspective from which they're presented. This awareness has made me a more critical consumer of information and a more intentional communicator.
